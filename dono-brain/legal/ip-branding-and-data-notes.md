# IP, Branding & Data Notes

Informal legal guidance on brand protection and data handling, distinct from
the Terms of Service work in [legal-terms-context-handoff.md](legal-terms-context-handoff.md).

### Trademark & Branding

- Brand identity (name, logo, colour scheme, font) is itself part of company value — keep an internal record of what's been settled on and treat it as informally "owned" before formal registration
- UK/EU trademark registration takes ~2–3 months and isn't too costly with an already-strong brand (EU trademark: ~£200–400)
- Before filing, search trademark directories to check for conflicts/likely confusion in our classification
- Consumer surveys are how disputes over brand confusion with an existing company get resolved in practice
- Trademark protection for AI-related branding/classification is currently unsettled law

### Confidentiality & Trade Secrets

- Keep internal records that prove authorship/originality of code and design (a verifiable "paper trail") in case of copying disputes
- Use confidentiality and non-compete terms in employee/founder agreements to protect trade secrets
- Directors carry significant personal responsibility — factor this into who holds that role

### Data & GDPR

- GDPR is manageable if scoped correctly: know what data you hold, why, and make sure it serves an actual, intended use — problems start when you retain data with no clear purpose
- Data can be retained indefinitely as long as it's genuinely still in use
- Aggregated insights from alumni donation behaviour can be shared with third parties only if fully anonymised first
- Avoid storing sensitive ID documents (e.g. driver's licences) for verification — a university ID/card number is a lighter-weight alternative
- Worth talking to a college development office contact about how they handle alumni data under GDPR, as a working precedent

### Tax

- Tax treatment differs meaningfully between the company and individuals — get advice from tax advisors, who will often help pre-incorporation for free
