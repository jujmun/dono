# Dono --- Company Context

> **Version 2.0 -- Foundational Company Context**

## Vision

Dono is the community infrastructure for transparent university giving.

We are building a mobile-first platform that enables young alumni to
collectively fund tangible improvements to student life. Rather than
donating into opaque institutional funds, donors can support specific
campaigns, follow their progress, and see the real impact of every
contribution.

Our long-term ambition is to become the default platform for trusted
community giving. Higher education is our first and most important
market.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Mission

To build lifelong communities of generosity by making university giving
transparent, social and rewarding.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# The Problem

## Students

Students, societies, departments and colleges regularly identify funding
gaps that are too small for traditional fundraising yet too large to
solve themselves.

Examples include:

-   Textbooks
-   Anatomy models
-   Musical instruments
-   Sports equipment
-   Conference travel
-   Welfare initiatives
-   Community events
-   Small capital improvements

Today these campaigns are fragmented across Instagram, WhatsApp, email,
newsletters and bank transfers.

## Young alumni

Young alumni remain emotionally connected to their university but are
underserved by existing fundraising models.

They:

-   have less disposable income than previous generations
-   prefer smaller but more frequent donations
-   expect transparency
-   want to know exactly where their money goes
-   want to remain connected to the community that shaped them

Traditional fundraising is optimised for major donors.

Dono is optimised for thousands of meaningful £10--£50 donations.

Supporting market data (UK giving decline, falling student disposable
income, trust gaps in where donations go): see
[market-research-notes.md](../research/market-research-notes.md).

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Our Core Insight

We believe people do not dislike giving.

They dislike giving without knowing what difference they made.

Trust in charities is not collapsing, but donors increasingly need
proof.

The key question is no longer:

> "Is this a good institution?"

Instead it is:

> "Where did my money actually go?"

Dono is built around visible, specific, low-opacity giving.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# What Dono Is

Dono is not simply a crowdfunding platform.

It is a community platform built around generosity.

Campaigns are the content.

Communities are the product.

The goal is not to maximise one-off donations.

The goal is to build lifelong communities of supporters who regularly
contribute to the places they care about.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Product Philosophy

Every product decision should reinforce these principles:

1.  Radical transparency.
2.  Small donations can have enormous collective impact.
3.  Giving should feel personal.
4.  Communities are stronger than isolated campaigns.
5.  Trust is earned through visibility.
6.  Generosity should become habitual.
7.  Every donor should feel that their contribution mattered.

# The Product

## Campaign creators

Campaigns may be created by:

-   Individual students
-   Student societies
-   Colleges
-   Departments
-   Universities

Campaigns focus on small-to-medium funding needs with clear, tangible
outcomes.

Examples include:

-   Textbooks
-   Equipment
-   Society projects
-   Conference travel
-   Sports equipment
-   Welfare initiatives
-   Accessibility improvements
-   Cultural events
-   Community outreach
-   Memorial funds

The platform is not intended to replace major university capital
campaigns.

Instead, it complements existing fundraising by supporting projects that
often go unfunded.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Trust Model

Trust is Dono's greatest competitive advantage.

Every campaign has multiple trust layers.

## Identity

-   Verified student
-   Verified society
-   Verified college
-   Verified university

## Institutional verification

Universities, colleges and verified societies can officially endorse
campaigns.

Verified campaigns receive an institutional verification badge.

## Community validation

Community engagement demonstrates demand through:

-   Likes
-   Donations
-   Followers
-   Comments
-   Activity

Popularity never replaces verification.

Institutional verification answers:

"Is this genuine?"

Community validation answers:

"Does the community care?"

# Competitive Advantages

Traditional university fundraising optimises for institutions.

GoFundMe optimises for emergencies.

Dono optimises for communities.

Uber aggregated drivers.

Airbnb aggregated hosts.

Spotify aggregated artists.

Dono aggregates university giving opportunities that are currently
fragmented across social media, email, newsletters, bank transfers and
generic crowdfunding platforms.

The community belongs to Dono rather than any individual campaign.

Oxford's own prior attempt at this, OxReach (built with consultancy Hubbub),
only ever listed 6 projects and skewed toward staff/academics rather than
students — see [market-research-notes.md](../research/market-research-notes.md#prior-art-oxreach)
for the precedent.

# Business Model

Revenue sources may include:

-   Donation transaction fees
-   Institutional subscriptions
-   Premium analytics
-   Additional donor features
-   Partnerships

Students will never pay to create campaigns.

Dono is a for-profit social venture, not a non-profit — advisor guidance was
explicit not to present otherwise. If demand for tax-deductible receipts
emerges, fiscal sponsorship (partnering with an aligned existing non-profit
for a small cut) is the fallback mechanism, not converting the company
itself. See [will-gregory-advisor-call.md](../research/meetings/will-gregory-advisor-call.md).
