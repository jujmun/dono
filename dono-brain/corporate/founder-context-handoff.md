# Dono Founder Structure & Incorporation Context Handoff
_Date: 16 July 2026_

## 1. What Dono Is

Dono is a mobile-first crowdfunding platform initially focused on universities and higher education communities.

### Core idea
Allow students, societies, colleges and university communities to raise money through transparent, community-driven campaigns.

### Target users
- Students
- Student societies
- Colleges
- Young alumni
- University supporters

### Long-term vision
Become the default platform for community giving within higher education.

Potential future expansion:
- US universities
- Fraternities/sororities
- Other educational communities
- Wider community fundraising

# 3. Major Constraint

UK Student visa rules potentially restrict:

- self-employment;
- acting as a company director;
- working for a company in which the student has a substantial beneficial interest.

The Home Office specifically references ownership of 10%+ as potentially problematic.

Current intended founder ownership is:

| Founder | Intended Equity |
|----------|----------------|
| Founder 1 | 20% |
| Founder 2 | 20% |
| Founder 3 | 20% |
| Founder 4 | 20% |
| Founder 5 | 20% |

Because the international founders intend to hold 20% each and are actively contributing technically, immigration advice is required.

# 4. Current Proposed Structure

## Incorporation

Preferred structure:

- UK Limited Company
- England & Wales

Reasoning:

- Initial market is UK universities.
- Lower complexity than Delaware.
- Easier for UK grants and investors.
- Easier regulatory alignment.

Potential Delaware flip later if major US investment requires it.

# 6. International Founder Plan

## Proposed mechanism

International founders do NOT immediately receive shares.

Instead they receive:

### Founder Option Agreements

These provide:

- Intended ownership: 20% each.
- Contractual right to receive shares later.
- Vesting schedule identical to other founders.

Potential triggers:

1. Obtaining a Graduate visa; OR
2. Permanently leaving the UK and continuing to work from abroad.

This maintains fairness while potentially reducing immigration risk.

---

# 7. Vesting Structure

Proposed structure:

- Four year vesting.
- One year cliff.
- Monthly vesting thereafter.

Example:

### If a founder leaves after:

6 months:
- Keeps 0%.

18 months:
- Keeps approximately 7.5%.

48 months:
- Keeps full 20%.

Reason:

Avoid dead equity.

Investors strongly prefer founder vesting.
