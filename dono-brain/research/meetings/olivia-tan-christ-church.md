### Fundraising Channels and Effectiveness

- Fundraising vehicles at Christ Church:
  - Events (with optional donation at sign-up)
  - Telethon: students call alumni over 1-2 weeks
  - Giving days: 36-hour digital fundraisers via email and social
  - Mail appeals and legacy mailings
  - Direct in-person donor meetings
- Efficacy ranking: in-person > video call > phone > all other channels
- Telethon pros and cons:
  - Pro: live back-and-forth builds rapport, concerns addressed on the spot, gift secured immediately with bank details
  - Con: expensive (14 students, accommodation, consultancy fees), antisocial hours, some alumni find calls intrusive
- Email is unreliable: auto-filtered to promotions, low open rates
  - Physical letters surprisingly effective, but recipients still procrastinate
- Biggest engagement barrier: simply getting hold of people, especially busy professionals with secretaries

### Donor Retention and Drop-off

- Most drop-off is operational, not emotional:
  - Legacy direct debits set up with end dates (now replaced by continuous DDs)
  - Credit card gifts expire when card does, no automatic notification sent
- Emotional cancellations do happen: financial hardship (redundancy, divorce, school fees), or bad institutional press
- Regular giving society: £10/month threshold; almost always set up via telephone
  - International donors use third-party platforms (no international direct debit)
- Personalization scales with gift size:
  - Lifetime giving recognition tiers: £10k, £50k, £100k, £500k, £1M+
  - Gifts over ~£10k get dedicated account manager and annual impact reports
  - Smaller donors receive fund-specific newsletters and mail-merge letters
- Most popular fund: college outreach, access, and student support (unrestricted, most flexible)
- Hardest area to fundraise for: graduate scholarships
  - Self-funding a DPhil costs £100k+; donors need convincing of its value vs. undergraduate study

### Org Structure and Recommendations

- Development office raises money; academic office distributes it (strict separation)
  - Societies must go through academic office (senior tutor, domestic bursar) for funding requests
  - A new discretionary fund was created ~2 years ago for things outside the JCR budget (subject dinners, furniture, etc.)
- Christ Church KPIs are long-term and flexible: no fixed annual target due to legacy gift unpredictability
- For comparable colleges, Olivia recommends Merton or St. John’s (similar alumni base and challenges)
- Also recommended: speak to graduate colleges separately, as their fundraising model differs significantly
- To find out about fund distribution: email the college’s generic office address (not a named individual), especially over summer
