### Worcester’s Fundraising Model

- Annual telethon + giving day (giving day skipped this year)
- Regular giving program brings in ~£250k/year, crucial as Worcester breaks even by ~£20k
- Direct debit setup via website for UK donors; card for international
- Work with Buffalo (fundraising consultancy) and possibly Toucantech
  - Toucantech not recommended: forces account login, adds friction
- Development team of ~7 people

### Targeting Young and Small-Ticket Donors

- Targeted recent leavers who don’t yet have a cemented regular giving program
  - Christ Church may be doing something similar
- Personal connections are key: student callers who can speak authentically
- Small, unrestricted gifts are especially valuable
  - Large gifts often restricted to specific projects, don’t cover operating costs
  - Regular small donations collectively keep the college running
- Donor pipeline logic: small donors become big donors, so can’t be dismissive of £5-20 gifts

### Stewardship and Impact Reporting

- Post-telethon: impact update sent ~1 month later on specific funds raised
- Summer mailing to all regular donors (video on college life areas supported)
- Tiered stewardship broadly:
  - Few thousand pounds+: individual stewardship (events, invitations)
  - £100+/year: invited to garden party
  - Threshold tied to HMRC gift aid rules (benefit can’t exceed tax reclaimed, ~25%)
  - General fund donors: periodic email updates

### Reaction to the Crowdfunding App Idea

- Key challenge: why would alumni visit the site over existing platforms like Crowdfunder?
- Vetting concern: development office can’t give students unfiltered access to alumni
  - Messaging and brand consistency must be controlled
- Traffic problem: can’t email 9,100 alumni every time a campaign launches
- Gravyty (US company) flagged as potentially doing something in this space
  - Worth investigating their crowdfunding-style model
- Worcester’s stance on societies fundraising independently: they’d want it to go through the development office
  - Won’t share alumni emails without permission
  - But willing to help on a case-by-case basis (e.g. rugby tour to Australia, targeted outreach to known supporters)

### Trends and Future of University Fundraising

- Telethon likely to evolve: moving toward smaller, more flexible calling campaigns tied to specific projects
  - Scam awareness is a growing concern, making cold phone asks harder
- Funding expected to become more project-based
  - Older alumni donate out of a sense of duty; younger alumni less likely to
  - Younger donors more inclined toward one-off, campaign-specific giving (feeds into the app concept)
- Broader trend: grant-making bodies tightening control and requiring more impact reporting
