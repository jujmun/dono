### Alumni Engagement and Donor Strategy

- Giving Day (March, library fundraising) outperformed previous years
  - Focused single narrative: accessibility, ethos, librarian perspectives, student voices
  - More new donors and higher total raised vs. prior multi-cause days
  - Key insight: a named, one-off project feels different to donors than an open-ended ask
- Regular donors can be overlooked in favour of large, “shiny” gifts
  - Some alumni giving consistently for years receive only generic reports
  - Plan: tiered recognition program for long-term regular givers (e.g. badges or acknowledgment at 5, 10, 20 years)
- Small donations often function as test gifts
  - Donors pilot a gift to assess stewardship, reporting quality, and trustworthiness before committing more
  - Good stewardship of small donors is critical: anyone in that pool could become a major donor
- Transparency builds trust with donors
  - Oxfam research: donors preferred honest reporting on challenges and delays over a “rosy picture”
  - College donors likely respond well to behind-the-scenes access and a sense of community, given the alumni connection
- Restricted vs. unrestricted donations: a common tension
  - Donors sometimes arrive with very specific funding ideas (e.g. “a computer scientist from Norway”)
  - Fundraisers work to broaden the brief or include a reversion clause to unrestricted funding
  - Smaller donors typically choose from three broad pools: student support, buildings/college, or academics/fellowships
- Competition with central university and departments managed via a shared database
  - College alumni are “primary prospects” for their college; first-contact rule applies for non-Oxford donors
