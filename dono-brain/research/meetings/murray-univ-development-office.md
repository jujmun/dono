### Donation Pipeline Overview

- Two main gift types: low-level recurring and major gifts
- Low-level recurring gifts
  - Generated via telethons (run by third-party firms) and giving days
  - Telethons target recurring monthly gifts, measured over a 4-year window
  - Giving days are one-off, online, project-specific (e.g. boat club, student support)
- Major gifts
  - Sourced through alumni events, CRM data (job titles, addresses, industries), and US-based screening firms
  - US screening leverages looser data regulations: property values, charitable giving records
  - Cultivation can take months to 5 years; relationship-building is the core driver
  - Donors often direct funds to tangible assets (buildings, named rooms) or subject-specific fellowships
  - Many give via pledges (e.g. £50k over 5 years with annual payments)
  - Current campaign: topping up tutorial fellowships whose endowments have been outpaced by inflation
- Legacies (third income stream)
  - Alumni leaving money in wills; unpredictable and reliant on data up to 50 years old
  - William of Durham Society holds an annual luncheon to maintain contact with legacy pledgers
  - Had a strong legacy year this year, significantly aiding the current campaign

### Donor Motivations and Stewardship

- Small-ticket donors: primarily motivated by community connection, nostalgia, and a sense of obligation to future students
- Large-ticket donors: motivated by recognition (naming rights) or pure philanthropy (anonymous giving)
- Donor designation
  - Both small and large donors can choose where their money goes
  - Giving day website presents multiple fund options (library, music, boat club, postgrad scholarships, etc.)
  - Telethon giving is steered toward “area of greatest need” (unrestricted income) for college flexibility
  - Major gift designation is established early in the cultivation process
- Stewardship
  - Impact reports provided mainly for institutional donors (e.g. Wilson Foundation: annual reports; Queens IR joint post: 6-monthly reports)
  - Trial 49 Society: regular givers of £12.49+/month invited to an annual luncheon
  - Less formal reporting than high-street charities; most alumni already understand college needs

### Online Donation Portal

- Live for just under 3 years (launched November)
- 157 gifts processed this year, up 24% year-on-year
- Donors can select specific funds via a dropdown; also used for event ticket booking
- Key advantage: donor covers bank fees optionally, saving the college ~£3,000 in fees
- Recently added recurring donation capability (previously one-time only)
- Main feedback: bank fee opt-in not clearly visible; ~5–10% of donors surprised by the higher total
  - College can and does refund the fee in those cases
  - Limited control over the display as it is an embedded link

### Student Society Donations

- College accepts donations to societies (drama, music, etc.) but does not actively promote them
- Current priority is deficit reduction: area of greatest need and tutorial fellowships take precedence
- Boat club is the one exception with active fundraising (dedicated giving day page, own web page with online giving form)
- Direct alumni-to-society giving: neither actively encouraged nor turned away
