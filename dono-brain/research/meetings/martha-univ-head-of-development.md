# Meeting with Martha (Head of Development for Univ)

Thu, 16 Jul 26

### Donation Process and Alumni Relations

- Relationship with donors usually pre-established through 3-6 years at college
- Donors can reach out directly via development office email or through the Master
- Donor pipeline generally moves small to large, but not always: some skip straight to major gifts, others leave large legacies with no prior contact
- Post-donation engagement is important: donors want to see direct impact
  - Giving day platform shows real-time breakdown of where funds go
  - Telethon donors receive thank-you videos
  - Annual “Futures” magazine outlines all fundraising and fund allocation

### Key Challenges and Friction Points

- Alumni fall off when a child is rejected by the college, or when the college does something publicly unpopular
- Small projects are no longer prioritized: finance committee now strictly dictates what the development office fundraises for
  - Previously more flexible; now focused on big-picture college priorities
  - Academics occasionally request small raises (e.g. £2,000) but the time cost is disproportionate
- Contact details for alumni are difficult to maintain, especially at non-collegiate universities (flagged as a potential opportunity)
- £2,000 is roughly the threshold where small-project fundraising becomes a problem for the development office
- Younger alumni rarely donate early: most start around age 30, peak giving in 50s-70s
  - Main barrier is simply lack of money
  - Young Univ is a social group (under-35s, mostly London events) used to keep young alumni engaged and details current

### Reaction to Student-Led Fundraising Platform Idea

- Univ unlikely to adopt due to strict finance committee oversight
  - Other, less structured colleges may be more open
- College verification/stamp-of-approval model also seen as potentially messy
  - Risk of fragmented funds and disproportionate admin burden
- Existing student involvement channels: telethon calling, development committee seats, Young Univ events
  - Young Univ did raise money for Univ North, but it was aligned to an existing college priority, led by a 34-year-old with a senior city job
- Most colleges already have straightforward online donation options; Univ’s was noted as particularly easy

### Additional Notes (from raw call notes)

- The college does work with student societies (e.g. rowing) on donations for specific needs
- Many young alumni already do regular giving on a monthly basis, commonly around £5/month
- Other, less collegiate UK universities are noted as generally weaker at alumni relations — alumni there report a more corporate, less personal relationship than the college system produces
- A structured route for students to raise what they need (e.g. students holding seats on the development committee) was floated as more palatable than an open platform

### Next Steps

- **Anonymize the college in any written materials or outputs**
  - Martha explicitly requested the college not be named in the project.
- **Relay findings to the team and reassess platform idea**
  - Univ is a poor fit; focus on less structured colleges as potential targets.
