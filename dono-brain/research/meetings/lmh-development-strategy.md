### LMH Development Strategy

- Anna is Director of Development at LMH (https://lmh.ox.ac.uk), ~10,000 alumni and supporters, ~13% donate in any given year
- Two-tier fundraising approach:
  - Mass fundraising: 2-3 figure gifts, monthly giving, managed by an annual fund officer
  - Major gifts: 6-7 figure donations, one-to-one relationship building (Anna’s focus)
- Giving days are the most current channel: gamified, 36-hour email blitz, social media push, match gifts
- Telephone campaign returning in September after a 4-year hiatus, tied to the 150th anniversary campaign
  - Previously dropped: cost ~£30-40k, net profit only ~£10-15k
  - Key issues: poor data quality (bad phone numbers) and low pickup rates
- Email effectiveness declining due to inbox saturation; hard copy still used for 70-75+ age group
- Peer-to-peer fundraising flagged as interesting but complex: data privacy, volunteer management, platform challenges
  - Anna has a 12-person volunteer board of major donors for the current campaign

### Payment, Restricted Funds, and Society Fundraising

- iRaiser: recently adopted donation form provider (used by major UK charities)
  - Replaced the old, clunky university-provided form; now supports Apple Pay and Google Pay
  - Fee covered by donor optionally, otherwise deducted from donation; no subscription model
  - Governance/trustee authorization was the slowest part of onboarding
- Restricted funds exist for boat club, choir, netball, law society, PPE society; QR codes route gifts to specific funds
  - Restricted funding is administratively burdensome at small amounts (separate fund setup, legal compliance, accounting overhead)
  - Workaround: “your £10 could fund X” framing rather than true restriction
- College prefers donations to flow through them: gift aid reclaim, accountability, and donor stewardship
- Societies fundraising independently is tolerated for parents/friends networks, but alumni outreach is politically sensitive
  - Anna has targets set by trustees; competing asks to the same alumni pool is a real concern
- Key tension for any third-party platform: colleges want visibility and control over who is being solicited and when
