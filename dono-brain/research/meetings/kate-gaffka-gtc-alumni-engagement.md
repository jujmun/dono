# Meeting with Kate Gaffka (GTC Alumni Engagement Team)

Fri, 10 Jul 26

### International Alumni and Tax-Effective Giving

- 60% of GTC students are international; 40% of alumni are overseas, and this will grow
- Tax-effective giving is cumbersome: each country needs its own agreement
  - Platforms in use: Benevity, Transatlantic Giving (TGSE)
  - Hong Kong is a top alumni hotspot but has no tax-effective giving solution
- Tailoring asks by country is also cumbersome: US messaging is very different from Southeast Asia, for example
- Kate’s view: if a donor really wants to give, they will, even without tax efficiency
  - More relevant for larger or more tax-advised donors; less of a barrier for smaller gifts

### Regular Giving and Cultural Differences

- UK has direct debits and standing orders for monthly donations; overseas does not
  - Makes consistent cash flow harder with 40% of alumni abroad
  - Americans give via regular credit card charges, a very different setup
- For international donors, bigger acquisition events (e.g. annual giving day) needed to retain donors rather than relying on recurring setups
- Recent giving day: raised £75k from 200 donors vs. £56k from 200 donors five years ago
  - Donor count stagnant, but average gift size rising significantly

### Donor Stewardship and Impact Reporting

- Good stewardship: always announce how raised money was spent
- Reporting thresholds at GTC:
  - Under £1,000: no specific impact reporting expected
  - £1,000–£10,000: general update; family trusts and foundations may ask more questions
  - £10,000+: full impact report (major donation threshold)
- Giving circles launching soon: tiered by gift size (regular giving, £1k–£15k, £1m+)
  - University giving circles start at £250k
  - Stewardship cycle feeds back into new asks

### Targeted Asks and Restricted Funding

- Focused medical teaching ask to medical alumni is an aspiration: something GTC could offer
  - Cuts the pool significantly, but alumni more likely to give to something they experienced
  - Contrast: history fellowship fundraise at another college was very hard; alumni moved into unrelated professions
- Restricting funds too narrowly is a real risk
  - Example: a large legacy restricted to students from Cornwall could never be spent; no catchment in the area
  - Preference for general “area of greatest need” pots unless donation is very significant
- Under £1,000: donors tend to accept flexibility; over £1,000: donors want specific allocation

### Crowdfunding, Peer Models, and Next Steps

- University of Southampton ran a successful crowdfunding platform around 7 years ago
  - Fallen out of fashion: relies too heavily on students, inconsistent across years
  - Hubbub were strong advocates but have moved away from it
  - Speak to Hubbub and Buffalo for cross-university insight
- Keble College did well with leavers’ gifts at graduation: strong conversion rate from recent graduates
- St Anne’s “Stanners Group” cultivating a pool of regular givers among recent leavers
  - Undergraduate colleges easier to target for this; postgrad colleges like GTC are harder
