# Milos Martinez Head of St Cross Development Office

Wed, 22 Jul 26

### Saint Cross Fundraising Overview

- ~6,000 alumni community; 220 donors in current financial year, £1.1M raised
- High-ticket donors (6 figures+): 1 donor; mid-tier (£10k–£25k): small handful; rest fall under £10k
- Participation rate currently ~5%, which is decent for a graduate college
  - Merton benchmark: ~17–18%; Univ has highest in Oxford at 30–40%
- Donations processed digitally; “make a gift” page stratified by country for tax efficiency
  - Key regions: US (Americans for Oxford), Switzerland, Hong Kong, China
- Age of alumni doesn’t suit a telethon approach; large international student population adds complexity

### Alumni Engagement Strategy

- Multichannel approach: postal appeals backed by social media and email follow-up
- Annual magazine, events program (Hong Kong, Singapore, San Francisco, Austin, Munich, Cambridge, Manchester this year)
- Career mentoring, private LinkedIn group, cheap/free events
- Alumni can become Common Room members for life, with access to facilities and lunches
- Cross Links fund: new interdisciplinary research award, first cohort of 3 projects funded this year
- Community Bursaries: 10 awards/year at £5,000 each for entrepreneurial or extracurricular students, backed by a major donor
- Strategy for early-career alumni: focus on participation over amount, then identify higher-level prospects as careers develop

### Platform Idea: Feedback and Suggested Next Steps

- Concept: student-led crowdfunding platform for small, concrete needs (e.g. textbooks, anatomy models, £50–£200 range)
  - Targets recently graduated alumni for £2–£5 micro-donations to build donor pipeline
  - Aims to make impact concrete vs. speculative appeal language
- Milos’s concerns:
  - Bias and inequality: students who present better may attract more funding
  - Alumni fatigue: Oxford alumni often receive appeals from multiple colleges already
  - Reputational risk: one poorly received campaign could damage the college’s image
  - Oversight: unclear how spend would be verified
- Suggested framing: embed as a sub-option within existing appeals (“here are specific student projects you can support”) rather than a standalone channel
- Saint Cross unlikely to partner in the near term (“ahead of the curve for us, still building the basics”)
- Colleges suggested as more open to trialing:
  - Somerville, Keble (innovative, push the envelope)
  - Balliol (long-standing development office, may want new ideas)
  - Merton (development director is approachable, though may not be the right fit)

### Next Steps

  - Get broader feedback on the platform concept before refining the pitch further.
- **Keep Milos updated on progress**
  - He's open to hearing how the idea develops, even if Saint Cross won't partner in the near term.
