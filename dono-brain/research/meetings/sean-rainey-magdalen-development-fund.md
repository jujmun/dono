# Meeting with Sean Rainey (Magdalen Development Fund)

### College Context

- ~9,000 alumni, ~10 staff covering them all
- Deliberately focuses fundraising on the ~200 alumni with the greatest capacity to give; not interested in small-ticket donors or building a pipeline from them
- Magdalen doesn't need the money — struggles to spend its student support fund most years — so a small-giving platform has little pull here
- First college to stop running telethons; expects poor telethon returns to push most colleges away from them eventually

### Views on Small vs Major Gifts

- Rejects the "major gifts grow from small gifts" pipeline theory from his own experience: the first gift a donor makes is usually already their biggest, not a stepping stone
- Would still run a small-giving program "if I had an unlimited budget" — for engagement, not revenue
- Major-gift donors are typically 40+; actively avoids asking anyone in their 20s for money, wants to shift that perception
- Believes strong alumni engagement (not small gifts) is what eventually builds a major-gifts program
- Prior donors are the best predictor of future donors; regular giving feeds bequests/wills

### Student Funding & Platform Reaction

- "The problem we have with raising money for student societies is the students" — inconsistent demand/interest and information from students themselves
- Colleges are barred from advertising undergraduate funding availability (would give richer colleges an unfair admissions advantage)
- Skeptical, but sees value in "ease of donation" — won't change outcomes fundamentally but would help
- Desperate to build a better relationship with the JCR; struggles to get student turnout (career days stopped due to no attendance)

### Alumni Behaviour Notes

- Donation rate is falling across Europe, North America and the UK even as total donation value rises
- "People like giving to people rather than institutions" — human stories outperform facts/figures
- GDPR restricts alumni data sharing, limiting UK alumni networks relative to the US
- Just hired a legacy-giving specialist and expanded the team; tracks alumni careers to spot future major donors
