### 1. About these Terms

1.1 These Student Campaign Terms apply to individual students who create or operate a Campaign on Dono ("you"). They supplement the Dono Terms of Service, which continue to apply, and take precedence over the Terms of Service where they conflict. Defined terms have the meanings given in the Terms of Service.

1.2 Dono is a trading name operated by Amrit Kaur Rooprai, a sole trader. Dono does not hold campaign funds, does not itself process card payments, and is not affiliated with any university, college or society unless expressly stated in writing.

1.3 If your Campaign is a Society Campaign, the Society Campaign Terms also apply and take precedence over these Terms where they conflict.

### 2. Eligibility

2.1 To create and operate a Campaign you must, at the time of creation and throughout the life of the Campaign:

(a) be at least 18;
(b) be currently enrolled at a Recognised Institution;
(c) be physically studying in the UK;
(d) hold and use a valid university email address;
(e) provide an acceptable student-card image;
(f) complete the Payment Provider's Connected Account onboarding; and
(g) remain eligible while the Campaign is active.

2.2 Visiting students and students on placement are eligible, provided they meet the other requirements.

2.3 The following are **not** eligible: recent graduates; people who have left their institution; people whose current student status cannot be verified; and students attempting to create a Campaign within 60 days of their expected graduation or departure.

2.4 Eligibility of the following categories has not yet been decided and, until confirmed, applications from these categories will be assessed individually and may be refused: suspended students; students on interruption; students on leave of absence; part-time or distance-learning students who are not physically studying in the UK; and students whose course ends before their formal graduation date. **[TO BE CONFIRMED BEFORE LAUNCH]**

### 3. Student-status checks, reverification and age

3.1 To create a Campaign you must submit a valid university email address and an acceptable student-card image. Dono administrators manually review the card and check your current student status. Dono aims to complete its review within seven business days.

3.2 The Payment Provider separately conducts its own identity and KYC onboarding for your Connected Account. Dono does not conduct those checks.

3.3 Your student status is reverified at the start of each academic year. Dono may request further information at any time; unless a different period is stated, you must respond within five working days **[TO BE CONFIRMED]**. Failure to respond may result in refusal, suspension or removal.

3.4 You declare your date of birth to Dono. Your student card is not relied on as proof of age. The Payment Provider's KYC process is intended to confirm identity and age eligibility. **[TO BE CONFIRMED — reliability of the age-check signal in the final implementation]**

3.5 The label "**Student status checked by Dono**" means only that Dono has completed the limited checks in this clause 3. It does not verify every campaign statement, the authenticity of every document, the accuracy of every cost, the viability of your Project, your future conduct, institutional endorsement, or successful completion.

### 4. Creating your Campaign

4.1 Your Campaign must include: a campaign name; a clear explanation of what is being funded and why the money is needed; a detailed itemised budget with accurate estimated costs; purchase links where applicable; a funding target; a Campaign End Date; an expected purchase or expenditure date; supporting media and documents where appropriate; a statement of who will own the funded property or output; and a planned update schedule.

4.2 You must accurately represent the Campaign in both the campaign application and the public campaign page, and must comply with the campaign rules and permitted categories in the Terms of Service and the Community Guidelines.

4.3 You must not fundraise on behalf of another individual and must not operate a pass-through campaign for another person or organisation. Fundraising on behalf of a Society is governed by the Society Campaign Terms.

### 5. Campaign duration and the Campaign End Date

5.1 Every Campaign must have a funding-closing date — the Campaign End Date — after which no further Donations may be made.

5.2 A Campaign may ordinarily remain open for no more than 12 months, and must ordinarily close at least 60 days before your expected graduation or departure. Dono may approve an extension at its discretion.

5.3 Donations are charged immediately and are collected even if the funding target is not met.

### 6. Partial funding

6.1 If your Campaign does not reach its target, you decide whether the Project can proceed with the amount raised.

6.2 If the money raised can still be meaningfully used for the stated campaign purpose, the Project may proceed. If it cannot be meaningfully used, you must arrange refunds in accordance with the Refund and Dispute Policy. Insufficient funding alone does not automatically cancel the Project.

### 7. Permitted use of funds and campaign changes

7.1 **Core rule.** Campaign funds may be used only for the purposes represented to Donors on the campaign page, including approved line items and reasonable directly related expenditure.

7.2 **Reallocation.** You may move funds between verified line items, provided the reallocation remains within the same stated campaign purpose. A material diversion to a different purpose is prohibited without Dono approval and any necessary donor notice or refund.

7.3 **Adding or changing line items.** You may: add a line item after publication, subject to Dono review; update a price where a source or reasonable evidence is provided; and buy a substitute item serving substantially the same purpose and costing a similar or lower amount.

7.4 **Material Changes.** A Material Change (as defined in the Terms of Service) requires, before implementation: an explanation; evidence or a source; Dono review; a campaign-page update; donor notice where appropriate; and may give rise to donor refund rights.

### 8. Surplus Funds

8.1 Funds may be moved between verified line items and used for reasonable additional expenditure directly advancing the stated campaign purpose. Any amount that cannot reasonably be used for that purpose must be refunded.

8.2 Surplus is refunded in reverse chronological order of donation: the most recent Donor first, up to the amount they donated, then the next most recent, until the surplus is exhausted. Surplus is not distributed proportionately. There is currently no de minimis threshold. Fee treatment is **[SUBJECT TO STRIPE CONFIGURATION]**.

### 9. Evidence and updates

9.1 You must declare when you plan to make purchases.

9.2 You must upload receipts or other evidence of expenditure within 14 days of each expenditure.

9.3 You must publish an outcome update approximately three months after expenditure.

9.4 Dono sends reminders before taking enforcement action. One missed deadline triggers follow-up by email and on-platform notice. Continued failure may result in suspension or removal of your Campaign or account.

9.5 The following remain open and will be confirmed in the published version: whether updates are also required every three months while fundraising; the general response deadline for information requests; whether evidence is public, donor-visible or Dono-only; what must be redacted from receipts; evidence-retention periods; and whether a final closure statement is mandatory. **[TO BE CONFIRMED]**

### 10. Funded property and resale

10.1 Unless the campaign page states otherwise, the funded item or output belongs to you and must be used for the stated campaign purpose.

10.2 An item must not be acquired with the intention of immediate resale or personal profit.

10.3 An item may not normally be sold within 12 months of acquisition without Dono's prior approval. Earlier sale may be permitted where the item is defective, unsuitable, replaced or no longer reasonably needed, for a documented reason.

10.4 If an item is sold before the campaign purpose is completed, the proceeds must be reapplied to that purpose or refunded. Disposal after genuine use for the campaign purpose is permitted.

### 11. Refunds, chargebacks and cooperation

11.1 Donors may seek refunds on the grounds and within the time limits set out in the Donor Terms and the Refund and Dispute Policy. You must respond to a refund request or related information request within five working days.

11.2 As the Connected Account holder, you are the Merchant of Record. Refunds and chargebacks are intended to be debited to your Connected Account, and the Payment Provider may apply its own recovery processes. **[SUBJECT TO STRIPE CONFIGURATION — negative-balance allocation, dispute fees and evidence handling]**

11.3 You authorise Dono to initiate a refund approved under the Refund and Dispute Policy where Dono considers this necessary. This is a last-resort power. **[SUBJECT TO STRIPE CONFIGURATION AND COUNSEL REVIEW]**

11.4 If an approved refund cannot be recovered from your Connected Account, you remain contractually liable for it. Dono never pays refunds from its own funds and does not pursue external debt collection, but Dono may: suspend or permanently ban you; remove your Campaigns; refer deliberate misuse to your institution; report suspected fraud to the police or another authority; and share relevant identity information with the Donor where lawful and reasonably necessary. The Donor may pursue chargeback, Payment Provider or legal remedies against you.

### 12. Graduation and departure

12.1 You must not start a new Campaign within 60 days of your expected graduation or departure.

12.2 Active Campaigns must close, complete or (in the case of Society Campaigns only) be properly transferred before you leave your institution. Individual Campaigns normally cannot be transferred.

12.3 Graduation or departure does not extinguish your refund, evidence, update, cooperation or investigation obligations.

### 13. Enforcement

13.1 Dono may warn, restrict, suspend, remove or ban in accordance with Part 10 of the Terms of Service, including for false information, fraud or suspected fraud, failure to provide evidence or updates, misuse of funds, unpaid approved refunds, Community Guidelines breaches, Payment Provider suspension or loss of student status. Dono cannot necessarily pause a payout already sent or recover money already withdrawn.

### 14. Your responsibility

14.1 You are responsible for: the truth and accuracy of your Campaign; your authority to run it; lawful conduct; costs and line items; proper use of funds; evidence; updates; the funded property and its disposal; refunds; chargebacks; cooperation with Dono and the Payment Provider; and claims caused by your fraud, illegality, misrepresentation or intellectual-property infringement.
