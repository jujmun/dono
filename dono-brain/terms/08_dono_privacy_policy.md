## 6. Your student card

6.1 We ask for an image of your student card so that an administrator can confirm you are currently enrolled at a Recognised Institution.

6.2 We extract from it only: your name, Institution, college, course, student number and card expiry date.

6.3 **The image itself is deleted six months after your most recent successful check.** The extracted details are kept for longer, as set out in clause 7, because they are what we actually need for eligibility, disputes and any institutional referral.

6.4 We do not ask for a passport, driving licence or other government identity document. Stripe conducts its own identity checks separately, and does not share those documents with us.

6.5 We do not retain barcodes, library identifiers, photographs of you beyond what is inherent in the card image before deletion, or other information on the card that we do not need. **[PRIVACY IMPLEMENTATION REQUIRED — confirm that the extraction process discards unneeded fields rather than storing the whole card record]**

6.6 Access to student card images is restricted to administrators who need it for verification, and every access is logged.

## 9. Institutions and development offices

9.1 Recognised Institutions are interested in the donations made to their students' Campaigns. Two quite different things may happen, and only one of them involves your personal data.

### 9.2 Aggregate insights — no consent needed, because it is not personal data

9.2.1 We may share with an Institution aggregated figures about **its own Campaigns only**: totals raised, numbers of donations, date ranges and averages.

9.2.2 We apply a **suppression threshold of five**. No breakdown is reported where fewer than five donors sit behind it; instead it is rolled up to Institution level. This is because a Campaign with a handful of donors is easily re-identified by anyone who saw the public page.

9.2.3 We do not share donor demographics.

### 9.3 Identifiable data — only if you opt in

9.3.1 We share your name, email address and your donations **to that Institution's Campaigns** with a Recognised Institution only where you have given separate consent.

9.3.2 We do **not** share your donations to other Institutions' Campaigns, or your donation history across the Platform.

9.3.3 That consent is: optional; unticked by default; separate from the Terms; specific about which Institution, what data and what purpose; and withdrawable at any time. **Refusing does not prevent you donating.**

9.3.4 **Once your data is transferred, the Institution becomes an independent controller.** It contacts you directly, from its own systems, under its own privacy notice. Dono does not send marketing on an Institution's behalf.

9.3.5 To withdraw consent, contact us — but note that we cannot delete data already held by an Institution. Our agreement with each Institution requires it to honour withdrawal requests, delete data on request and not share it onward, and we will pass your request on. You may also contact the Institution directly.

9.3.6 The exact fields shared, and the consent wording, are **[TO BE CONFIRMED BEFORE LAUNCH]** and must be settled alongside the Institution Agreement. **[COUNSEL REVIEW REQUIRED — UK GDPR consent standard and PECR direct marketing]**

## 10. Anonymity

10.1 You may choose to donate **publicly anonymously**. This means your name is hidden from the public Campaign page. Your donation amount is still shown.

10.2 **Publicly anonymous is not completely anonymous.** Stripe has your payment data. Dono holds your account and donation records. Whether the Campaign Owner sees your name and email is a separate choice you make at checkout.

10.3 You may change your anonymity setting at any time, in either direction. Changing it affects future display only — it cannot recall what a Campaign Owner has already seen, or anything already screenshotted, cached or archived.

10.4 Comments always display a name. If you donated publicly anonymously and then comment, you will identify yourself.

10.5 In a refund dispute, your identity is withheld from the Campaign Owner by default where you donated publicly anonymously or ask for it to be withheld. See clause 5.3 of the Refund and Dispute Policy.

## 11. Published receipts

11.1 Campaign Owners publish evidence of how they spent donated money.

11.2 Receipts must be redacted by the Campaign Owner before upload, following a checklist that requires removal of home addresses, contact details, account and order numbers, card details and third-party names. Dono reviews the first receipt from any Campaign Owner before publication and samples thereafter.

11.3 Unredacted originals are held privately and are never published.

11.4 If you believe a published receipt contains personal data that should not be there — yours or anyone's — tell us and we will unpublish it immediately while we investigate.
