### 3. What your Donation is — and is not

3.1 A Donation is a donation or gratuitous contribution to the stated campaign purpose. It is **not**:

(a) an investment;
(b) a loan;
(c) equity or an ownership interest;
(d) a payment for goods or services;
(e) a guarantee of any outcome; or
(f) automatically repayable because you are dissatisfied with the outcome.

3.2 Donations are not necessarily tax-deductible. Dono does not issue charitable tax receipts and Gift Aid is not claimed on Donations. Taxes are your responsibility.

3.3 Campaign Owners are students or societies, not Dono. Dono does not guarantee the identity of any user beyond its limited published checks, or the authenticity, accuracy, viability, completion or outcome of any Campaign.

### 4. Who receives your money

4.1 Your Donation is processed by the Payment Provider (Stripe) and paid to the Connected Account of the Campaign Owner. The Connected Account holder is the Merchant of Record. Dono receives only its platform fee.

4.2 For Society Campaigns, the campaign page discloses whether the legal recipient is a society account or a named student's account.

### 9. Partial funding, changes and surplus

9.1 If a Campaign does not reach its target, the Campaign Owner decides whether the Project can proceed. If the money raised can still be meaningfully used for the stated campaign purpose, the Project may proceed and your Donation is retained. If it cannot be meaningfully used, the Campaign Owner must arrange refunds.

9.2 Campaign Owners may move funds between verified line items within the same stated campaign purpose and make limited changes under the Campaign Terms. Material Changes require Dono review, a campaign-page update and donor notice where appropriate, and may give rise to refund rights.

9.3 **Surplus Funds.** Any amount that cannot reasonably be used for the stated campaign purpose must be refunded. Surplus is refunded in **reverse chronological order of donation**: the most recent Donor is refunded first, up to the amount they donated, then the next most recent, until the surplus is exhausted. Surplus is **not** distributed proportionately across all Donors, and no Donor is guaranteed a proportionate surplus refund. There is currently no de minimis threshold. **[SUBJECT TO STRIPE CONFIGURATION — fee mechanics]**

### 10. Refunds

10.1 **Grounds.** You may request a refund through Dono's internal process on grounds including: duplicate payment; payment error; unauthorised payment; campaign cancellation; material misrepresentation; fraud; misuse of funds; failure to provide required evidence; a material unauthorised change; inability to use the money meaningfully for the stated purpose; failure to proceed where funds should therefore be returned; or verification failure. Mere dissatisfaction with the outcome is insufficient.

10.2 **Time limits.** An ordinary refund request must be made within 60 days after the Campaign End Date, or within 60 days after you became aware of the alleged breach, where later. A request based on fraud or deliberate material misrepresentation may be made up to 12 months after the Donation.

10.3 **Process.** Either party may submit evidence, including receipts, photographs, purchase records, financial information, campaign updates, communications, and links or pricing sources. The Campaign Owner has five working days to respond to a request. Dono may decide on no refund, a partial refund or a full refund. Dono's decision deadlines are **[TO BE CONFIRMED BEFORE LAUNCH]**.

10.4 **Appeals.** You may appeal Dono's initial decision within ten working days. The appeal is reviewed by a different administrator where reasonably practicable.

10.5 **Fee treatment.** In broad terms: if a campaign cannot proceed because it did not raise enough, Dono refunds its own fee but not the Payment Provider's fee; if the Campaign Owner caused the refund through mistake, breach or misrepresentation, you should receive Dono's fee back and the Campaign Owner bears the economic cost where technically possible; if you caused the refund through your own error, you bear the non-refundable Dono fee; for partial refunds, Dono's fee may be refunded proportionately where you were not at fault. The published Refund and Dispute Policy will state exactly what you receive, who bears the cost, and what the Payment Provider can technically reverse. **[SUBJECT TO STRIPE CONFIGURATION]**
