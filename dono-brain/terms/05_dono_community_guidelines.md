# Dono Community Guidelines

**Version:** Working draft v0.1 — 20 July 2026
**Status:** DRAFT — NOT FOR PUBLICATION. Contains unresolved markers. Requires UK solicitor review, including Online Safety Act analysis, before use.

---

### 1. Purpose and scope

1.1 These Community Guidelines apply to all content and conduct on Dono, including campaign pages, comments, images, documents, usernames and reports. They form part of the Dono Terms of Service and apply to every user — students, societies, donors and institution users.

1.2 Dono is a platform for genuine student fundraising within a university community. These Guidelines exist to keep the platform honest, respectful, lawful and safe.

### 2. Core principles

2.1 Be honest: campaigns and comments must not mislead. Be respectful: disagreement is fine; abuse is not. Be lawful: nothing on the platform may facilitate or promote illegal activity. Be safe: do not endanger, exploit or target others.

### 3. Prohibited content and conduct

3.1 The following are prohibited anywhere on the platform:

(a) illegal activity, or content facilitating or promoting it;
(b) fraud and scams;
(c) misleading fundraising, including false or exaggerated campaign claims;
(d) harassment, threats or abuse;
(e) discrimination or hateful content;
(f) impersonation of any person, society or institution;
(g) spam;
(h) sexual or pornographic content;
(i) grossly offensive content;
(j) exploitation of any person;
(k) dangerous or regulated activity;
(l) copyright infringement;
(m) trademark infringement;
(n) unauthorised use of institutional branding, including university and college names and logos;
(o) false claims of affiliation or endorsement;
(p) ban evasion; and
(q) malicious reporting.

3.2 Dono may remove content that it reasonably considers contrary to these Guidelines.

### 4. Campaign integrity

4.1 Campaigns must comply with the campaign rules and permitted categories in the Terms of Service and the applicable Campaign Terms. In particular, do not: create duplicate or deceptive campaigns; fundraise on behalf of another individual; run pass-through campaigns; plagiarise another campaign; or claim a verification, approval or endorsement you do not have.

4.2 Badges have precise meanings. "Student status checked by Dono", "Stripe onboarding completed", "Society approved" and "Institutionally endorsed" each describe a specific, limited check or approval, explained in the Verification Policy. Do not misdescribe them.

### 5. Comments

5.1 Dono has no private messaging. All account holders may post comments, and all comments are public.

5.2 You may edit or delete your own comments. The interface indicates when a comment has been edited.

5.3 Campaign owners may remove a comment from public display on their own campaign, but may not edit another user's wording. Dono retains a private moderation copy and audit log, and may restore a comment where campaign-owner removal is misused.

5.4 There is currently no blocking feature. Use the reporting tools instead.

### 6. Reporting

6.1 Any user may report content or conduct they believe breaches these Guidelines, using the on-platform reporting tools or by emailing **dono.outreach@gmail.com**.

6.2 Reports should identify the content and briefly explain the concern. Malicious or knowingly false reporting is itself a breach of these Guidelines.

### 7. Moderation and enforcement

7.1 Moderation occurs after publication. Dono reviews reported content and may also review content proactively.

7.2 Where Dono reasonably considers these Guidelines have been breached, it may: issue a warning; remove or hide content; restrict comments or other features; temporarily restrict the visibility of a campaign during an investigation; suspend an account; remove a campaign; or permanently ban a user. Dono may also refer matters to a recognised institution and report suspected crime to the police or another authority, in accordance with the Privacy Policy.

7.3 Enforcement action under these Guidelines is separate from, and may be taken alongside, action under the Terms of Service, the Campaign Terms and the Refund and Dispute Policy.

### 8. Appeals

8.1 You may appeal a moderation decision, including removal of your content, removal of a comment, removal of a campaign or suspension of your account, by contacting **dono.outreach@gmail.com**.

8.2 Appeal deadlines and reviewer arrangements for moderation decisions are **[TO BE CONFIRMED]**. Appeals against internal refund decisions are governed by the Refund and Dispute Policy (currently ten working days from the initial decision).

### 9. Changes

9.1 Dono may update these Guidelines as the platform develops and to comply with law, including the Online Safety Act **[COUNSEL REVIEW REQUIRED — in-scope analysis, illegal-content risk assessment and any additional duties]**. Material changes will be notified in accordance with the Terms of Service.
