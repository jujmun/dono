## How to complete this document

The developer needs to answer four questions:

1. **What cookies and similar technologies does the Platform actually set?** Load the site in a clean browser, open developer tools, and list every cookie and every item in local and session storage — name, purpose, first or third party, and expiry.
2. **Is there any analytics?** If the answer is "none yet", this document becomes very short and no consent banner is needed.
3. **Is there a consent management tool?** If not, one is needed before any non-essential cookie is set.
4. **What does the authentication provider set?** Session cookies are usually essential, but tokens that persist across sessions may not be.

Until those are answered, clause 4 stays empty.

## 1. What cookies are

1.1 Cookies are small files placed on your device when you visit a website. Similar technologies — local storage, session storage, pixels and software development kits — do comparable things. In this Policy, "cookies" covers all of them.

1.2 This Policy explains which ones Dono uses, why, and how you control them. It sits alongside the Privacy Policy, which explains what we do with personal data generally.

---

## 2. The law we work to

2.1 Under the Privacy and Electronic Communications Regulations 2003, we may set cookies that are **strictly necessary** for a service you have asked for without your consent. Everything else — analytics, performance measurement, advertising — requires your consent before it is set.

2.2 Consent must be freely given, specific and informed. It must be as easy to refuse as to accept, and as easy to withdraw as to give.
